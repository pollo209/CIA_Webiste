CIA Application Process

The Consumer Insights and Analytics Major and Minor at LSBE prepares select students to contribute to the success of organizations by optimizing the relationship with their customers through data-driven insight and modeling. Using a unique non-traditional learning environment, you are given the opportunity to apply fundamental, technical, qualitative, and quantitative analysis techniques in developing your personal methodology for analyzing data and providing insight.

Program Expectations

The Consumer Insights and Analytics major/minor are selective. Students accepted must commit to:

  • Maintain a minimum GPA of 3.0.
  • Demonstrate a keen interest in qualitative and quantitative analysis.
  • Spend considerable time in the CIA Lab to supplement classroom learning and develop strong data management and analysis skills using Excel, PowerBI, Tableau, R, Python and other software through independent practice.
  • Develop personal leadership skills and those in the cohort by being willing to share knowledge and experiences with fellow CIA students.
  • Participate in student CIA program meetings outside of class and be willing to attend special Consumer Analytics industry seminars, competitions, conferences, and community events.

Program Application Process

Sophomores interested in the major are strongly encouraged to meet with the CIA Program Director. An initial informational meeting or review of program opportunities helps interested students determine whether or not to pursue formal application and the appropriate prerequisite courses. The application and selection process involves a thorough review of each candidate's application and academic performance, as well as a formal interview. Applicants are reviewed and selected according to their skills, academic qualifications and "fit" with the program in terms of career goals, diversity, and interests. Candidates are expected to maintain high ethical standards.
